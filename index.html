ABOUT ME

  • Hi! I am a scientist, biker and an amateur baker.

    I studied at the University of Toronto, focussing on how mutations can rewire the genome and its impact on phenotypes. I am now in the Bay Area at Hexagon Bio, developing novel strategies to target cancer using functional genomics, pooled screening technologies and genetic screens.

    When I'm not running experiments, you can find me biking in the local hills, tackling a classic segment or biking with Alto Velo. In my spare time, I like to find cool places to bike around the world. I also like to think about how to build tools to help better model experiments in the lab, namely through vibe coding.

    Fun fact: I've lived in 4 of the top 20 most expensive cities in the world so far.
